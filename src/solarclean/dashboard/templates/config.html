Configuration essentials

Site location, timezone, and period

The knobs most runs actually change. Applying writes a validated YAML edit into the source below — coordinates, both timezone fields, DST-correct period offsets, and (optionally) new whole-day period dates in one step.

What the location changes
Applying the map position updates site.latitude, site.longitude, both timezone fields, and DST-correct whole-day period offsets (00:00–23:00); the location's IANA timezone is detected automatically from the coordinates. With nasa_power, it also changes the fetched and cached hourly GHI/DNI/DHI, air temperature, wind, humidity, and precipitation. Rainfall, seasonal soiling, event dates, and cleaning decisions then follow that weather and local-time grouping; humidity-coupled soiling (dew cementation) and coating condensation follow the fetched weather when enabled.
What stays fixed
Dust-event frequency and the Riyadh central-v2 cost calibration do not change with the selected coordinates. Apply the location to the YAML source, then validate and save as usual.
